In 1977, the coastal village of Broad Haven in Pembrokeshire became the epicentre of one of Britain's most prolific UFO flaps. On 4 February, fourteen primary school children independently drew near-identical pictures of a cigar-shaped silver craft that had landed in the field beside their school. The wave continued throughout the year — hotel owner Rosa Granville reported two tall humanoid figures near a landed disc in April — with over 450 paranormal incidents reported to local authorities in twelve months, earning the area the nickname the Welsh Triangle.
