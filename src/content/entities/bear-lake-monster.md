Bear Lake straddles the Utah–Idaho border in turquoise improbability,
and ever since white settlers arrived in the 1860s the lake has been
said to hold something serpentine. Mormon settler Joseph C. Rich's 1868
articles in the *Deseret News* described a creature with stubby legs, a
crocodile head, and a body fifty feet long. Rich later confessed to
embellishment, but Indigenous traditions of the Shoshone preserved their
own warnings about the lake long before he wrote.

The Bear Lake Monster sightings have continued, sporadically, into the
twentieth and twenty-first centuries. The lake is deep, cold, and very,
very blue.
