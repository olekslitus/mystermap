Colapesce (Cola the Fish) is one of the oldest and most beloved figures in Sicilian legend, documented as far back as the 13th century. A boy who loved the sea more than land gradually transformed — growing scales, webbed hands, and the ability to breathe underwater. He became the eyes beneath the waves for King Frederick II, reporting on sunken ships and the geology of the seabed. On his final dive he discovered that Sicily itself rested on three columns, one broken, and stayed behind to hold it up — explaining the island's earthquakes as the shudder of his effort.
