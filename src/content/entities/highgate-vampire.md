In late 1969 and early 1970, multiple witnesses reported a tall, dark figure with hypnotic red eyes gliding through Highgate Cemetery in North London, prompting a bitter public rivalry between self-proclaimed vampire hunters David Farrant and Sean Manchester. On the evening of 13 March 1970, after ITV broadcast interviews with both hunters, a mob of hundreds armed with stakes and crucifixes broke into the locked cemetery to confront the creature — one of the most dramatic outbreaks of vampire hysteria in modern British history. Folklorist Bill Ellis later analysed the affair in the journal Folklore as a textbook case of ostension, in which circulating rumours inspire real-world enactments.
