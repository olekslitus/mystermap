In 1948 the people of Alton, Illinois began reporting an enormous bird
overhead — a wingspan in feet that ran to the double digits, somewhere
between a small plane and a fairy tale. The sightings spread from
Alton outward.

The most famous of them happened on July 25, 1977 in Lawndale, when ten-
year-old Marlon Lowe was, his mother insisted, lifted briefly off the
ground by one of two enormous birds in the family's yard. Marlon was
unhurt; the birds flew off; the story went national. American
"thunderbird" sightings borrow the name (and only the name) from far older
Indigenous traditions of giant raptor-spirits.
