According to Charles Godfrey Leland's 1899 publication, Aradia was sent by the moon goddess Diana to teach the oppressed peasants of Tuscany the art of witchcraft — a form of spiritual resistance against feudal lords and the Church. Whether the 'Gospel of the Witches' records genuine Tuscan folk-religion or is substantially Leland's invention remains hotly debated among folklorists. Regardless of its origins, the text became one of the most influential documents in the birth of modern Wicca, carrying the image of Italy as a land of ancient witchcraft into the 20th century.
