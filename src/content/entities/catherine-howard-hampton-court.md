Catherine Howard, fifth wife of Henry VIII, was arrested for adultery in November 1541 and executed at the Tower of London in February 1542. Legend holds that on learning of her impending arrest she broke free from guards and ran screaming down the Haunted Gallery to reach Henry VIII, who was at prayer in the adjoining chapel. Staff and visitors have reported an anguished screaming female figure in the gallery for decades; in October 2003 a CCTV camera captured a figure in period costume flinging open a fire door on three consecutive nights, footage released by Historic Royal Palaces and covered worldwide.
