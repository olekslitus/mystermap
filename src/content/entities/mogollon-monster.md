The Mogollon Rim is a 200-mile escarpment that runs across central Arizona, dividing the high country from the desert. It is heavily timbered, deeply forested, and almost empty. Since 1903 it has also been the home, by reputation, of a tall, hair-covered, foul-smelling biped — Arizona's Bigfoot.

The earliest published account is by sportsman I.W. Stevens, who described in the Arizona Republican of October 4, 1903 a creature with long matted hair and claws that he encountered along a remote canyon. Stevens gave the creature a description that Bigfoot literature would adopt almost word-for-word seventy years later.

Reports along the Rim continued through the twentieth century, especially around Payson, Heber, and Christopher Creek. Hunters describe footprints in the snow. Forest Service workers describe knockings in the trees at dusk. The BFRO database lists dozens of cases.

The Mogollon is not as famous as the Pacific Northwest. The country is harder to walk through, and the storytelling has a different rhythm — quieter, more local, more dry pine.
