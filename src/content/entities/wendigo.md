The Wendigo (or Windigo) is a being from Algonquian traditions across the
northern woods — Cree, Ojibwe, Innu, and others — long predating any
settler chronicle. It is described as a gaunt, ravenous spirit associated
with cannibalism, winter starvation, and an insatiable greed that
transforms the eater into one of its kind.

The 1907 entry on this map marks a documented Western-press sighting near
Lake Windigo, Minnesota, where lumberjacks reported a tall horned figure
walking along the treeline. The tradition itself is older, deeper, and
not anyone's to "discover." We mark its territory here because that is
where the cold woods are.
