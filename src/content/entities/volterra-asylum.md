The abandoned psychiatric hospital of Volterra sits on a dramatic Tuscan hilltop where Etruscan, Roman, and medieval history overlap. At its peak the facility held over 6,000 patients. When Italy closed its psychiatric institutions in 1978, the complex was simply left to decay. Today it is one of Italy's most infamous urban exploration sites, with numerous accounts of unexplained phenomena in its crumbling wards.
