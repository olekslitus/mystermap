Built by the brilliant and controversial Emperor Frederick II around 1240 CE, Castel del Monte rises alone from the Apulian plateau in a form unlike any other medieval castle: a perfect octagon flanked by eight octagonal towers. It has no water supply, no kitchen, no stables, and was never used militarily. Scholars have proposed it functioned as an astronomical instrument, a symbol of imperial and cosmic power, or an initiation site connected to esoteric traditions. It remains one of Italy's most mysterious monuments.
