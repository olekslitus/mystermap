The Ozark Howler — Ozark Black Howler in some tellings — is a Missouri-Arkansas cryptid the size of a bear, the shape of a wolf-cat hybrid, and (witnesses agree) crowned with two short horns. The eyes, witnesses also agree, glow red. The cry, which is what gives the animal its name, has been variously described as a steam-whistle, an elk in distress, or 'a woman screaming on the ridge above you.'

The earliest verbal references date to the 1880s, when Ozark hill farmers spoke of howlers killing livestock at night. The modern wave of sightings, in print, begins around 1980 in the upper Buffalo River country.

In the late 1990s a self-described cryptozoologist circulated emails about the Howler that were eventually traced to a University of Arkansas student playing an elaborate prank. The hoax, however, did not produce the sightings; it only borrowed them.

The Ozarks are old hills, much-dissected, full of cave systems and dry hollows. The Howler keeps its territory there. The cry, when it comes, is unmistakable, and very, very close.
