According to Somerset legend, a malevolent witch once inhabited the limestone caves at Wookey Hole, blighting cattle, souring butter, and cursing young lovers; the villagers appealed to Glastonbury Abbey, and a monk climbed to the caves armed with holy water, turning the witch to stone — a stalagmite formation still visible today. The earliest historical reference appears in William of Worcester's 1480 account, which described the formation as a woman clothed and spinning, without yet naming her a witch. Archaeological work in 1912 by Herbert Balch uncovered bones, a stalagmite ball, and an alabaster knife near the first cave chamber, lending a veneer of physical evidence to the tale.
