In April 1976, two young sisters on holiday near Mawnan Smith, Cornwall, were so frightened by a huge winged figure hovering over the tower of Mawnan Old Church that their father cut the holiday short. Subsequent encounters through the late 1970s and into the 1990s described a humanoid standing four to five feet tall with large red eyes, grey feathered wings, and pincer-like clawed feet, often seen hovering silently above the ancient churchyard. Sceptics have attributed it to an escaped Eagle Owl, yet independent witnesses who were unaware of the legend reported near-identical details.
