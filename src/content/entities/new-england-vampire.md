Tuberculosis killed slowly, in clusters, and consumed entire families
through the eighteenth and nineteenth centuries in rural New England.
The disease was not understood. So the explanation that grew, here in
the back roads of Connecticut and Rhode Island and southern Massachusetts,
was that the dead were rising at night to feed on the living.

The remedy was to exhume the most recently dead family member; if the
heart still appeared fresh, it was burned to ash, and the ash sometimes
fed to the dying as a cure. The last documented case is the 1892
exhumation of Mercy Brown in Exeter, Rhode Island, two years after
Bram Stoker had begun research for *Dracula*. Some scholars think
Mercy's case directly informed the novel.
