In March 1995, livestock began dying mysteriously in Orocovis, Puerto
Rico — drained of blood, with two puncture wounds at the neck. Witnesses
described a kangaroo-shaped, spined, red-eyed creature about three feet
tall: the *chupacabra* ("goat-sucker"). The story spread north and west
within a year, into Mexico, Texas, and across the American Southwest.

The "Texas chupacabra" turned out, in case after case, to be coyotes
suffering from severe sarcoptic mange. The mythology, however, has
proven more durable than the explanation: the chupacabra remains the
single most common cryptid sighting reported across South Texas every
summer. Whatever it is, it is still drinking the goats.
