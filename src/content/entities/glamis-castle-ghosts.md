Glamis Castle, ancestral seat of the Earls of Strathmore and childhood home of Queen Elizabeth The Queen Mother, is associated with at least four distinct haunting traditions. The Grey Lady is believed to be Janet Douglas, Lady Glamis, burned at the stake for witchcraft in 1537, whose chapel seat no one in the household dares occupy. Earl Beardie's ghost is said to play cards eternally in a sealed chamber after wagering his soul with the Devil on the Sabbath. The Monster of Glamis, rumoured to be a hideously deformed heir hidden in the 19th century, was first publicly discussed in the 1840s.
