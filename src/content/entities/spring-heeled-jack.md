Spring-Heeled Jack is one of Victorian England's most enduring mysteries: a tall, diabolical figure capable of superhuman leaps, clad in a tight oilskin suit and helmet, with eyes that burned red as fireballs and hands tipped with iron claws. The first wave of attacks in London in 1837-38 caused enough public alarm for the Lord Mayor to open an official inquiry, and witnesses included respectable women who described being clawed and burned by blue flames. Sightings spread across the country over the following decades, with notable clusters in the Midlands and Sheffield in the 1870s and bizarre appearances at Aldershot Army Barracks in 1877 when sentries fired live rounds at a leaping shape without apparent effect.
