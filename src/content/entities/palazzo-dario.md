Known as the 'House of No Return,' Palazzo Dario is a jewel-encrusted 15th-century palazzo on the Grand Canal with an unbroken five-century record of tragedy. Owners and their associates have died by violence, suicide, or sudden illness at a rate that has made the property all but unsellable. The curse is attributed by some to the dedicatory inscription on its facade, which inverts the usual blessing formula.
