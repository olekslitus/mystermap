In early July 1947, rancher W.W. "Mac" Brazel found a field of strange
debris on the Foster Ranch outside Roswell, New Mexico. The local Army
Air Field issued a press release announcing the recovery of a "flying
disc." A day later, the Air Force walked it back: weather balloon. Decades
later, declassified documents revealed the actual answer — Project Mogul,
a top-secret balloon array designed to detect Soviet nuclear tests.

None of which mattered. By the late 1970s, witnesses had come forward with
stories of crash debris, alien bodies, and a government cover-up. Roswell
became the world's UFO capital, and is still where the American story of
extraterrestrial contact is rooted.
