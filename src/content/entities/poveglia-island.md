A small island in the Venetian Lagoon with one of Europe's darkest histories. Used as a plague quarantine station from 1793 to 1814, it received over 160,000 dying victims whose remains were burned and buried in mass graves — soil on the island is said to be composed largely of ash and bone. Later converted to a psychiatric hospital until 1968, the island has been abandoned ever since. Local fishermen refuse to approach it.
