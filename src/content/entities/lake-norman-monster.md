Lake Norman is a relatively new place. Duke Energy flooded the Catawba River valley to fill it in 1962, and within months the first sightings of a long-necked, serpentine creature began surfacing in the local press. North Carolinians have called it Normie ever since.

The LakeNormanOfNC website maintains an informal sightings database that, as of 2019, listed 21 reports stretching from the 1960s to the 2010s. Witnesses describe a body twelve to fifteen feet long, a head that breaks the surface like a snorkel, and a wake that fishermen know is not a boat.

The lake is large — 32,475 acres, with 520 miles of shoreline — and deep in places, but it is also man-made. There is no plausible refugium of relict aquatic life. Skeptics suggest large catfish, beavers, and the occasional flooded tree. Believers point out that the lake covers what used to be river bottom and pasture, and that what was buried there is anyone's guess.

Normie has not surfaced for the cameras yet. The lake is busy.
