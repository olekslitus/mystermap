In the winter of 2003–2004, the small Sicilian coastal hamlet of Canneto di Caronia became the focus of international attention when dozens of fires broke out with no identifiable source — televisions, fridges, sealed boxes, and wiring all igniting spontaneously. The Italian government deployed a multi-disciplinary team that spent months investigating and failed to reach a consensus. Proposed causes ranged from electromagnetic anomalies to methane seepage to outright poltergeist activity. The case remains officially unresolved.
