In May 1971, the Ford family of Fouke, Arkansas reported a seven-foot
hairy creature with red eyes attempting to enter their farmhouse through
a window. Bobby Ford was scratched on the back; the creature was shot at
and ran. The story drew newspaper coverage, then a low-budget 1972 film —
*The Legend of Boggy Creek* — that grossed astonishing money for its
budget and turned the swamps along Boggy Creek into a permanent point on
the cryptid map.

Sightings continued through the 1970s and 1990s. Locals are fond of saying
that the monster never goes away — it just stops coming around for a while.
