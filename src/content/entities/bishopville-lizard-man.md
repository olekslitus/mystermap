In the early hours of June 29, 1988, seventeen-year-old Christopher Davis
stopped along a road near Scape Ore Swamp in Bishopville, South Carolina
to fix a flat tire. By his account, a seven-foot bipedal creature with
green scales, three-fingered clawed hands, and red glowing eyes ran from
the trees and tried to climb onto his car as he drove away. The car had
deep scratches and a damaged side mirror.

The Lizard Man stayed in the news for months — a Sheriff's department
investigation, a $1 million bounty, plaster casts of three-toed prints
in the swamp mud. Sightings have surfaced sporadically since, most
recently in 2015. Lee County leans into the legend each summer.
