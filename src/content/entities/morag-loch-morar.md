Loch Morar in the Scottish Highlands is Britain's deepest inland water, plunging to over 1,000 feet — deeper even than Loch Ness — and is said to harbour its own resident monster named Morag. Sightings date to at least 1887 and describe a large creature with rough brownish skin, three humps, and a small head on a slender neck. The most dramatic modern encounter came in August 1969 when two fishermen struck the creature with their boat in the dark; when it remained alongside them, one fired a rifle at it and it sank. The Loch Morar Survey of 1970 collected 34 eyewitness reports dating back to the nineteenth century, giving Morag a more systematically documented dossier than almost any other British lake monster.
