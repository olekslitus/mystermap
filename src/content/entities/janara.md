The Janara are the legendary witches of Benevento, a city in Campania with a reputation for the dark arts stretching back over a thousand years. Named for the priestesses of Diana, they were believed to gather on the banks of the Sabato River beneath a sacred walnut tree, performing rites connected to the ancient goddess cult. The 1428 trial of Matteuccia di Francesco in Todi introduced the Benevento witches to the wider European witch-trial tradition, and the walnut tree became the archetypal meeting place for the Italian Sabbath.
