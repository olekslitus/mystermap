Am Fear Liath Mor — the Big Grey Man — is a hulking presence said to haunt the summit plateau of Ben Macdui, Scotland's second-highest peak. The first publicly documented account came in 1891 when respected mountaineer Professor Norman Collie confessed to hearing crunching footsteps behind him in the mist — each one matching three or four of his own — and being seized by such overwhelming terror that he ran for miles without stopping. Subsequent witnesses, including naturalist Dr A.M. Kellas and climber Frank Smythe, described a vast grey-brown shape striding through blizzard conditions, sometimes ten feet tall. Whether the phenomenon is a Brocken Spectre, infrasound-induced panic, or something else entirely, the mountain continues to inspire dread in solo walkers to this day.
