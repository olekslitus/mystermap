Black Shuck is a spectral black dog said to roam the coastline, heathland, and country roads of Norfolk, Suffolk, and Essex. Its name likely derives from the Old English scucca, meaning demon or fiend, and references appear in chronicles as far back as the twelfth century. On 4 August 1577 the creature reportedly burst through the doors of St Mary's Church in Bungay during a violent thunderstorm, killing two worshippers and leaving scorch marks on the north door that can still be seen today. Modern witnesses consistently describe a dog the size of a calf, jet-black fur, and a single enormous glowing eye that precedes misfortune or death within a year.
