Lake Iliamna is the largest lake in Alaska, big enough to hide what
locals have called Illie since at least the early twentieth century. A
1963 sighting by biologist Gary Smith — twenty-five to thirty feet long,
gray-blue, moving with what looked like flippers — pulled the creature
into the scientific press for a moment.

Some hypothesize a population of Pacific sleeper sharks. Some hypothesize
freshwater white sturgeon, which are not known to live in Iliamna but
could. Some hypothesize Illie. The lake is over a thousand feet deep at
its deepest, and we have inventoried very little of it.
