The story begins in 1735, when Mother Leeds — depending on whose telling —
either cursed her thirteenth child or simply gave birth to a creature with
a horse's head, a goat's body, leathery wings, and a forked tail. The
newborn flew up the chimney and into the Pine Barrens, where it has lived
in some form ever since.

The Devil entered the historical record in earnest during the week of
January 1909, when more than a thousand people across South Jersey reported
seeing the same creature in barns, schools, and trolley tracks. Schools
closed. Mills shut down. The sightings have never quite stopped, and the
million-acre Pine Barrens remain one of the densest, darkest forests on
the eastern seaboard.
