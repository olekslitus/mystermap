The Surrey Puma is a large cat, described as tawny or sandy-coloured and roughly the size of an Alsatian, reported repeatedly across the hills and farmland of Surrey since the late 1950s. Unlike the Beast of Bodmin, the Surrey Puma attracted serious investigative attention early: in 1964 police followed tracks and found a freshly killed deer, and livestock losses were officially attributed to a large felid. In August 1966 paw prints were cast in plaster and assessed by London Zoo staff as consistent with a puma. The Dangerous Wild Animals Act of 1976 was partly prompted by Britain's epidemic of big-cat escapes in the preceding decade, though no Surrey specimen has ever been captured.
