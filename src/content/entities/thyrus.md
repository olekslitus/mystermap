The Thyrus is a medieval dragon whose legend is inseparable from the city of Terni in Umbria. Named after an ancient name for the Tiber river, the creature was said to haunt the marshy lowlands surrounding the city, making it unsafe until a local knight slew it. So central is the Thyrus to Terni's civic identity that its image appears on the official coat of arms of the city to this day.
