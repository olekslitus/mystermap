In Diné (Navajo) tradition, a yee naaldlooshii is a witch who can take the
shape of an animal — most often a coyote, wolf, or owl — to do harm.
The being is bound to the land, named only carefully, and not a subject
of casual storytelling.

The territory marked here is the rough Four Corners region of the Navajo
Nation. The fact that "skinwalker" has become a cryptid celebrity in
non-Native media — most famously around the so-called Skinwalker Ranch in
Utah's Uinta Basin — is a tension this site does not resolve. We mark the
land. We cite the people who live on it. We try to keep our hands clean.
