The Shroud of Turin is a 4.4 × 1.1 metre linen cloth bearing a faint double image of a man with wounds consistent with crucifixion. First documented in 14th-century France and transferred to Turin in 1578, it is venerated by millions as the burial cloth of Jesus Christ. Scientists have struggled for decades to explain how the image was formed — no painting technique, photographic process, or medieval technology has been demonstrated to replicate it convincingly. A 1988 carbon-dating test pointed to a medieval origin; subsequent challenges to that result have kept the debate alive.
