The South Bridge Vaults were built into the nineteen arches of Edinburgh's South Bridge when it opened in 1788, initially serving as workshops and taverns before being abandoned to the city's poorest inhabitants and eventually sealed. Rediscovered in the 1980s, the vaults have become one of the UK's most visited paranormal sites, with investigators reporting cold spots, poltergeist activity, and apparitions including a child known as Jack, a violent presence called Mr Boots, and a young girl called Annie. A 2003 Most Haunted broadcast brought the vaults to national attention and dramatically increased paranormal tourism to Edinburgh.
