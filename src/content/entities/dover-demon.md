Over forty-eight hours in late April 1977, three teenagers in the affluent
suburb of Dover, Massachusetts independently reported seeing the same
small creature: about four feet tall, hairless, with thin spindly limbs
and a watermelon-shaped head whose enormous orange eyes "glowed like
marbles." Their drawings, made independently, matched.

The creature has never been seen since. The case is unusual for being
short, geographically tight, well-documented, and presented by witnesses
who never sought money or fame from it.
