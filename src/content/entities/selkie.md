Selkies are mythological seal-people of Orkney and Shetland who shed their skins to walk on land as men or women; if a human steals the skin, the selkie is compelled to remain ashore, often as an unwilling spouse. The Orkney archipelago holds more selkie narratives per square mile than any other region in the Celtic world, with tales documented in virtually every island. Seventeenth-century Orkney records also document sightings of Finn-men — strange figures in unusual vessels — which modern scholars suggest were Inuit hunters in kayaks blown off course, real encounters that fed the legend.
