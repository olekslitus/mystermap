Lake Garda is the largest lake in Italy, reaching depths of 350 metres in its northern section. Since 1965 a steady stream of witnesses — including groups of tourists — have reported sightings of a large brown creature moving through its waters. Nicknamed Bennie after the lake's Latin name Benacus, the entity is typically described as serpentine, humped, and significantly longer than any known freshwater fish in the region.
