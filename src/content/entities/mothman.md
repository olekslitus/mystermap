In November 1966, two young couples driving past an abandoned World War II
munitions plant outside Point Pleasant, West Virginia reported a winged
humanoid with glowing red eyes keeping pace with their car at speeds over
100 mph. Their account opened a thirteen-month wave of sightings — over
a hundred witnesses, by some counts — that lasted until December 1967, when
the Silver Bridge across the Ohio River collapsed during rush hour and
killed forty-six people.

For believers, Mothman was the omen. For folklorists, he was a regional
panic given a face. Either way, Point Pleasant adopted him: the town now
hosts an annual festival, a museum, and a 12-foot stainless-steel statue
of the creature in the town square.
