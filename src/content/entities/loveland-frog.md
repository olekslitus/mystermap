The Loveland Frog made its print debut in 1955, when a businessman driving
a deserted road outside Loveland, Ohio reportedly saw three short, frog-faced
humanoids standing by a bridge over the Little Miami River. One held what
looked like a wand. He drove on.

In March of 1972, two Loveland police officers — independently, on
different nights — described the same creature crossing the road and
climbing over a guardrail near the river. Two more sightings followed in
2016. Loveland keeps the frogman as a town mascot, and the bridges over
the Little Miami still feel like a place where something might be watching.
