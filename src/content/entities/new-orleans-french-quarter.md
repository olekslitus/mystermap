The French Quarter — the Vieux Carré — is roughly seven blocks by twelve,
and it is impossible to walk a single one of them without passing a
building somebody has, at some point, sworn was haunted. The 1814 entry
on this map marks the era of the LaLaurie Mansion, where Madame Delphine
LaLaurie is alleged to have tortured the people she enslaved on the upper
floors; in 1834 a fire revealed the cruelty, and the building has been
called haunted ever since.

The voodoo queen Marie Laveau, the pirates of the Lafitte's Blacksmith
Shop, the dancers of the Hotel Monteleone — the Quarter is layered with
death, jazz, and devotion in roughly equal measure. To live there is to
live with the company of strangers from many decades.
