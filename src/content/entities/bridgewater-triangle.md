Cryptozoologist Loren Coleman coined the term 'Bridgewater Triangle' in 1983 to describe a roughly two-hundred-square-mile patch of southeastern Massachusetts — anchored on the towns of Abington, Freetown, and Rehoboth — that, by his calculation, hosts an unusually high density of every kind of paranormal rumor that this country produces.

Inside the Triangle: bigfoot reports along the Hockomock Swamp, thunderbird sightings around Rehoboth, ghost-light wisps over the Bridgewater bogs, satanic-cult rumors in the Freetown-Fall River State Forest, UFO sightings throughout. Most date to the twentieth century. Some are older.

The Wampanoag people, whose ancestral lands cover the area, called the Hockomock 'the place where evil spirits dwell.' Colonial diaries from the 1760s describe ghost-lights over the swamp. King Philip's War — the bloodiest war per capita in American history — was fought partly on this same land in 1675.

The Triangle is not a tourist destination. The state forest is open. The swamp is not. People who live near it tend to keep their porch lights on.
