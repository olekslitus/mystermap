Beginning in late 1982 and peaking in 1983–1986, residents across
Westchester, Putnam, and Dutchess counties in New York — and across the
state line into western Connecticut — reported a massive, silent,
boomerang-shaped craft drifting low over the suburbs. Witness counts ran
into the thousands. Reports came from off-duty police, pilots, and IBM
engineers.

A small group of pilots later admitted to flying tight formations of
ultralight aircraft with synchronized lights as a prank. That accounts
for many sightings. It does not, witnesses say, account for all of them —
particularly those that describe a single solid object the size of a
football field, low above the trees.
