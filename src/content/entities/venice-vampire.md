During Venice's catastrophic plague of 1576 — the same epidemic that killed Titian — gravediggers on the quarantine island of Lazzaretto Nuovo encountered a suspected vampiric threat: a woman whose burial shroud had decayed and partially entered her mouth, a phenomenon interpreted at the time as evidence that she was 'eating' her way back to life. The solution was to force a brick between her teeth. Her skeleton, discovered by archaeologists in 2006 and analyzed in 2009, constitutes the first physical evidence of anti-vampire burial ritual ever found.
