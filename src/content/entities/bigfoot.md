The modern Bigfoot era begins in August 1958, when bulldozer operator
Jerry Crew found enormous humanlike footprints around his work site near
Bluff Creek in northern California. A local newspaper coined the name
Bigfoot. Nine years later, on the same creek, Roger Patterson and Bob
Gimlin filmed roughly a minute of jerky 16mm footage of a tall, hair-covered
biped now seared into American memory.

Indigenous traditions across the Pacific Northwest had named such beings
for centuries — Sasquatch, Ts'emekwes, Skookum — long before the term
"cryptid" existed. The Cascade and Klamath ranges, ancient and rain-thick,
remain the densest cluster of reported encounters anywhere on the continent.
