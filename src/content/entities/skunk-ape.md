The Skunk Ape is what Florida calls Bigfoot — though the regional details matter. It is described as smaller, slighter, and overwhelmingly malodorous, smelling (witnesses agree) of methane and rot. Its preferred habitat is the cypress strand and saw-grass slough of the Big Cypress and Everglades.

The modern wave of sightings began in 1974, when hunters working the swamps north of the Tamiami Trail brought back stories of a hairy biped that left footprints in the muck. The Florida Department of Game and Fresh Water Fish briefly investigated.

In 2000, an unsigned letter and two photographs arrived at the Sarasota County Sheriff's office. The pictures showed what looked like a large primate at the edge of a property, eyes glowing. The 'Myakka photographs' have never been fully explained.

David Shealy runs the Skunk Ape Research Headquarters out of Ochopee on the Tamiami Trail, where the gift shop has held up better than the science. The swamps remain — vast, hot, and full of footprints that someone, eventually, will find.
