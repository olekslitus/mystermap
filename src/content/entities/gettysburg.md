Over three days in July 1863, between 46,000 and 51,000 men were killed,
wounded, captured, or went missing at Gettysburg. It was the deadliest
battle of the Civil War. Mass graves were dug and re-dug for years
afterward.

The ghost stories began almost immediately. Reenactors describe being
joined by men in unfamiliar uniforms who vanish at the treeline.
Visitors to the Devil's Den, the Wheatfield, and Little Round Top photograph
figures that were not there. The National Park Service does not
endorse the stories, but they do not deny them either; they only ask that
the dead be respected. Most visitors do.
