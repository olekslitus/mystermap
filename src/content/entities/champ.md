Champ is North America's most-witnessed lake monster: a long-necked,
serpentine, plesiosaur-shaped shadow seen rising in Lake Champlain. The
French explorer Samuel de Champlain has been credited (probably wrongly)
with the first sighting in 1609; either way, the lake's Indigenous
peoples — the Abenaki and the Iroquois — had long known of *Tatoskok*
or *Gitaskog* before any Frenchman arrived.

There are over 300 logged sightings, plus sonar contacts, plus the famous
1977 Sandra Mansi photograph of something cresting the surface. Both
Vermont and New York have laws on the books prohibiting the harming of
Champ.
