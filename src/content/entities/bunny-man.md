On the night of October 19, 1970, Air Force Academy cadet Robert Bennett pulled his car to the side of Guinea Road in Burke, Virginia to talk with his fiancée. A figure in a white rabbit suit — they would tell police — emerged from the woods, threw a hatchet through the front windshield, and shouted something about trespassing.

Fairfax County police took the report seriously. Two weeks later, a security guard at a nearby construction site reported a man in a bunny costume on the porch of a partially-built house, again wielding a hatchet, before fleeing into the woods. Both reports made it into the official county log.

The story expanded. By the 1980s the legend insisted that the Bunny Man was an escaped inmate from a long-closed Lorton asylum (which never existed); that he hung children from the rafters of the Colchester Road railroad overpass; that the bridge — now widely called Bunny Man Bridge — turns red on Halloween at midnight.

The folklore is excessive. The original two reports are real. The bridge is still there, on a quiet road, in a county that long ago stopped being rural.
