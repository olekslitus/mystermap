Beginning in 1817, the family of John Bell on a Robertson County, Tennessee
farm reported being tormented by an invisible entity that called itself
"Kate." She slapped, pinched, and pulled the children's hair; she
quoted Scripture verbatim from two distant churches simultaneously; she
spoke fluently with visitors. Andrew Jackson is reputed to have spent a
night at the farm in 1819 and left, supposedly, by sunrise.

John Bell died in 1820, and the entity took credit. She left in 1821,
promising to return in seven years and again in a hundred. Her cave on
the farm is still there, still visited, and Adams, Tennessee remains the
quiet center of America's most famous family haunting.
