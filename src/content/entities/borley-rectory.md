Built in 1862 for the rector of Borley parish, the rectory became the subject of Britain's most extensively documented haunting after psychic researcher Harry Price arrived in 1929 and catalogued testimony from some 200 witnesses describing over 2,000 incidents. Reported phenomena ranged from a phantom nun and a headless coach to wall-writings appealing for help. The rectory burned down in 1939 and was demolished in 1944; a subsequent Society for Psychical Research inquiry found much of the evidence unreliable, but Borley retains its reputation as England's most famous haunted site.
