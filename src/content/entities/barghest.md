The Barghest is a shapeshifting spectral creature of northern English folklore, most commonly encountered as a massive black dog with fiery eyes, enormous teeth, and clanking chains, but also capable of assuming the forms of a headless man, a white cat, or a rabbit. Sightings are concentrated across Yorkshire, Durham, and Northumberland; the creature is said to roam the narrow medieval snickelways of York and the remote limestone gorge of Troller's Gill in the Dales, where according to an 1830 ballad a man was found dead with inhuman marks upon his body. Those who see it clearly are said to die soon afterwards.
