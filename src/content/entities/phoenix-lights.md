On the evening of March 13, 1997, thousands of people across Arizona
watched a triangular formation of lights move silently south across
the state, from the Nevada border to the Mexican line. A second event
later that night — a hovering arc of flares above Phoenix — produced
the iconic photographs.

The Air Force eventually attributed the late-night event to A-10 Warthogs
dropping flares during a training exercise. The earlier formation has no
agreed-upon explanation. The governor at the time, Fife Symington, played
the incident off in public, then admitted twenty years later that he
himself had seen the craft and that it was "otherworldly."
