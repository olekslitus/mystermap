The kelpie is the most widespread water spirit in Scottish folklore: a shapeshifting creature that most often appears as a powerful black horse beside a loch or river, whose back is supernaturally adhesive so that anyone who mounts it cannot dismount before being dragged beneath the surface to drown. The creature can also assume human form, though it betrays itself by water weeds in its hair or reversed hooves. Place-name records from Kirkcudbright dated 1674 provide one of the earliest written traces, while the Loch Ness kelpie tradition pre-dates the modern monster legend by centuries.
