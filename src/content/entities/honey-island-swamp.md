The Honey Island Swamp covers roughly 70,000 acres along the Pearl River
on the Louisiana–Mississippi border. In 1963, a retired air-traffic
controller and his hunting partner reported encountering a seven-foot,
gray-haired bipedal creature with amber eyes; later they returned and
made plaster casts of three-toed prints. The casts were unusual: webbed,
heavy, and consistent over multiple visits.

The local explanation involves a circus train derailment in the early
twentieth century from which a chimpanzee escaped and bred with the
swamp's alligator population — biologically impossible and beloved
anyway. Honey Island remains thick, hot, and very, very full of things
that move at night.
