Pluckley, a village of around 1,000 residents in the Weald of Kent, was dubbed England's most haunted village by local resident Frederick Sanders in a 1950 Kentish Express article listing ten resident ghosts. The Guinness Book of Records recognised it in 1989 with twelve reported hauntings. Academic research published in the journal Folklore in 2025 traced several legends to real historical incidents — a woman burned to death in 1911, a quarry death in 1899, and a suicide in 1919 — while finding that three stories were entirely invented for a 1962 TV Times article.
