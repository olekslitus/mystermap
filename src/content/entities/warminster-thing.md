Beginning on Christmas morning 1964, residents of Warminster in Wiltshire began reporting a bizarre combination of crushing sonic attacks and luminous aerial craft. Local journalist Arthur Shuttlewood of the Warminster Journal coined the term The Thing and became the phenomenon's chief chronicler, eventually claiming to witness around 800 sightings himself. Cradle Hill on the town's northern edge became a globally known sky-watching destination, drawing thousands of visitors throughout the late 1960s and 1970s, with over 1,000 individual UFO reports logged in a single year. No conventional aircraft or weapon was ever identified as the source of the sonic attacks.
