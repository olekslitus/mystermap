Lake Como, the third-largest lake in Italy and one of the deepest in Europe, has been the site of persistent reports of a large unidentified creature since the mid-20th century. Nicknamed the Lariosauro after the Triassic-era fossil Lariosaurus balsami found nearby in 1830, the creature is consistently described as reptilian, scaled, and several metres long. The lake's extraordinary depth — over 400 metres in places — lends plausibility to the idea that large undiscovered fauna could inhabit its waters.
