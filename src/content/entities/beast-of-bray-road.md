The earliest report places it on a rural road outside Elkhorn, Wisconsin
in 1936: a six-foot wolf-like creature seen briefly by a night watchman.
The Beast went quiet for half a century, then returned in the late 1980s
and through the 1990s, when nearly a dozen drivers along Bray Road
reported the same animal — bipedal, with a snout, sometimes carrying
roadkill in its forepaws.

Cryptozoologist Linda Godfrey collected and published the cases. The
sightings cooled by the early 2000s but have never entirely stopped.
