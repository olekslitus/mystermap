In 1955 the CIA selected a dry lakebed in the Nevada desert — Groom Lake —
for the secret development and testing of the U-2 spy plane. The base
that grew there was officially nonexistent until 2013, when a FOIA-released
CIA history finally acknowledged it.

Test flights of the U-2 (and later the SR-71 and the F-117) at impossible
altitudes, doing impossible things, were repeatedly reported by Nevada
pilots and citizens as UFOs. The Air Force was happy to let the
"flying saucer" explanations stand. Decades of cover and denial calcified
into the mythology that endures: aliens, recovered craft, hangars beneath
the salt.
