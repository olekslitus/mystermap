Anne Boleyn, second wife of Henry VIII, was beheaded on Tower Green on 19 May 1536 and buried in the Chapel of St Peter ad Vincula. Her headless apparition has been reported across multiple sites within the Tower complex for centuries, making her Britain's most widely witnessed royal ghost. Sightings range from a gliding white figure on Tower Green to a torchlit procession inside the locked chapel at night.
