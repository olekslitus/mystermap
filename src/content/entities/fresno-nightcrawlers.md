In 2007, a Fresno homeowner reviewed his front-yard CCTV footage and
found two thin, white figures crossing the lawn. They had no arms and
no torsos to speak of — just two extremely long legs, a small head, and
a lurching, bipedal walk that looked like a pair of trousers given
locomotion. They walked off camera and out of his yard.

Similar footage surfaced from Yosemite in 2011, then Poland in 2018.
The Fresno Nightcrawler is now a beloved figure of internet folklore,
adopted by the Choctaw Nation as a recognized "guardian," and one of the
quieter cryptids on this map: it does not menace, it does not speak.
It just walks past your house at three in the morning.
