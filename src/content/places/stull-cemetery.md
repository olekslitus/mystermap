Stull is a tiny unincorporated community in Douglas County, Kansas,
population around twenty. Its hilltop cemetery, founded in 1857 next to
a small stone Evangelical United Brethren church, would be unremarkable
if not for an article in the *University Daily Kansan* on November 7,
1974, which reported that the cemetery was one of the seven gates to
Hell on Earth.

The article cited no source. It quoted no expert. It was, by all
appearances, a Halloween-tinged bit of college-newspaper invention.
But it caught. Pope John Paul II's 1995 flight, the rumor went, was
re-routed to avoid airspace over Stull. (The Vatican has never
confirmed.) On Halloween 1988, an estimated 150 thrill-seekers gathered
at the cemetery; the local sheriff began enforcing trespassing laws
the next year.

The church burned in 2002. The cemetery is still posted against
trespassing. The story is unkillable.
