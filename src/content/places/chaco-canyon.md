For three centuries, beginning around 850 CE, the people now called the
Ancestral Puebloans built and inhabited the great sandstone complexes of
Chaco Canyon: Pueblo Bonito, Pueblo del Arroyo, Chetro Ketl, and a dozen
others. The largest contained over 600 rooms and rose four to five
stories. Roads as straight as Roman roads radiated outward into a system
spanning much of the Four Corners.

The buildings encode astronomical alignments at the level of fanaticism.
The Sun Dagger atop Fajada Butte tracks the summer and winter solstices
and the lunar standstill cycle. Pueblo Bonito's two main walls align
to true north and to the equinox sunrise. The accuracy is not chance.

Around 1150 CE — possibly because of a long drought, possibly other
reasons — the Chacoans left. They walked, taking their door lintels and
their roof beams with them. Their descendants are the modern Pueblo
peoples of the Rio Grande and Hopi Mesas. The ruins are silent now in
a way that visitors find difficult to describe.
