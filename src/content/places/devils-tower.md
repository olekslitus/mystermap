The Lakota call it Matȟó Thípila — Bear Lodge. The Cheyenne, Crow,
Kiowa, and Arapaho all have their own names and their own stories of
how the tower's vertical columnar fluting was made: most involve a giant
bear scratching at the column as children climbed for safety. The
columns are real. They are fifty million years old.

Theodore Roosevelt declared Devils Tower the United States' first
National Monument in 1906, partly to keep climbers from defacing it.
The Indigenous nations who hold the site sacred have long asked
climbers to refrain in June, when ceremonies take place.

In 1977, Steven Spielberg's *Close Encounters of the Third Kind* used the
Tower as the landing site for a benevolent alien craft. Visitor numbers
increased fourfold the following summer. The aliens, by all reports,
have not returned.
