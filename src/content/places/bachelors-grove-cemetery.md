Bachelor's Grove is a small abandoned cemetery in the Rubio Woods Forest
Preserve southwest of Chicago. It was established in 1864 by German and
English settlers, fell into disuse by the 1960s, and is now reachable
only by a footpath through the preserve. It is also, by reputation,
one of the most haunted cemeteries in the United States.

The accounts include: phantom farmhouses that appear and dissolve,
a small black two-door car driven by a long-dead family, lights moving
between the trees at the back of the cemetery, and a woman in white
seated on a tombstone, photographed in 1991 by a Ghost Research Society
volunteer. The photograph is often called the Madonna of Bachelor's
Grove. The Society has investigated more than a hundred separate
incidents.

The cemetery has been heavily vandalized. Bodies have been disinterred,
tombstones moved or stolen. What is haunted, the long-time investigators
will tell you, may not be the dead so much as the living memory of
how they were treated.
