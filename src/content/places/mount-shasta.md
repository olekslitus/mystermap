Mount Shasta has been sacred to the Karuk, Modoc, Wintu, and Shasta peoples
since long before any white observer arrived to rename it. Since the late
nineteenth century it has also been the imagined home of the Lemurians —
robed survivors of a sunken Pacific continent — said to live in a
crystalline city called Telos beneath the mountain's south slope.

Frederick Spencer Oliver's 1894 novel *A Dweller on Two Planets* introduced
the lore in print. By the 1930s, the I AM Activity (a religious movement
founded by Guy and Edna Ballard) had relocated Saint Germain himself to
the mountain. Since then, every flavor of New Age teaching has at one
point claimed the slopes: ETs, ascended masters, gateways to inner Earth.

Climbers report compass anomalies. Pilots report lenticular clouds that
do not behave like clouds. The mountain itself is sublimely indifferent.
