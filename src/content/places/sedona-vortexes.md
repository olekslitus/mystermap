In 1980, a self-described psychic named Page Bryant identified four sites
around Sedona — Bell Rock, Cathedral Rock, Boynton Canyon, and Airport
Mesa — as "vortexes" where the planet's spiritual energy spirals up out
of the red sandstone. Within ten years Sedona had become America's
single largest New Age destination.

The science is what you would expect: the rocks are iron-rich and
beautiful and give bad cell signal. The believers will tell you that this
is precisely the point. Channelers, crystal healers, soul retrievers,
past-life regressionists, and an entire economy of guided vortex tours
have made the town one of the highest-cost-of-living small cities in
the American West.

The Yavapai people, whose ancestral land Sedona occupies, were aware
the place was special long before Page Bryant. Their stories are
different. They are also less commercially available.
