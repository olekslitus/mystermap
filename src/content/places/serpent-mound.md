The Great Serpent Mound is a coiled effigy mound 1,348 feet long, three
feet high, twisting along a ridge in southern Ohio. The serpent's mouth
is open around an oval — sometimes interpreted as an egg, sometimes a
sun, sometimes the head of a frog the snake is about to swallow.

Radiocarbon dates have ranged wildly: some samples place construction
around 320 BCE (Adena culture); others around 1070 CE (Fort Ancient).
The site itself sits at the edge of a Cryptoexplosion structure — a
geological scar likely caused by a meteorite roughly 320 million years
ago. The builders almost certainly did not know this. They almost
certainly did know the ridge was unusual.

The serpent's coils align with key points on the solstice and equinox
horizon. New Age tourism here is enthusiastic; archaeology is more
cautious. The mound, regardless, has not been moved.
