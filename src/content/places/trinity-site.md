At 5:29 AM on July 16, 1945, in the Jornada del Muerto desert valley
of southern New Mexico, the first atomic bomb in human history
detonated. The 21-kiloton plutonium implosion device, codenamed Gadget,
left a crater 2.4 meters deep and fused the surrounding sand into a
glassy green-and-black layer the scientists later called *trinitite*.

J. Robert Oppenheimer, watching from base camp ten miles south, later
quoted the Bhagavad Gita: "Now I am become Death, the destroyer of
worlds." Brigadier General Thomas Farrell wrote in his report that the
explosion produced a light "equal to several suns at midday."

The site is on the White Sands Missile Range and opens to the public
twice a year, the first Saturdays of April and October. Visitors are
warned not to take trinitite home. Many do. The ground itself is still
mildly radioactive, and the place — by every metric of the supernatural
the rest of this map applies — is the most haunted single location on
the continent.
